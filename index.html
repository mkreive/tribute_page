Frida Kahlo

One of the most significant artists of the twentieth century

In Cooperstown, N.Y., an exhibit at the Fenimore Museum looks at the photographic portraits of Frida Kahlo snapped by her sometime lover Nickolas Muray.

"The twenty-first-century Frida is both a star – a commercial property complete with fan clubs and merchandising – and an embodiment of the hopes and aspirations of a near-religious group of followers. This wild, hybrid Frida, a mixture of tragic bohemian, Virgin of Guadalupe, revolutionary heroine and Salma Hayek, has taken such great hold on the public imagination that it tends to obscure the historically retrievable Kahlo."[247] – Art historian Oriana Baddeley on Kahlo
